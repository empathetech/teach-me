The Two-Mode Rule

The architectural split that defines the whole curriculum.

The two modes look and feel different on purpose. Each one signals what to do with the page.

LEARN mode

🧘 Magazine prose

For the why. Read on the couch, before you cook.

  • Cool sage tint background
  • Generous prose width (~64ch)
  • Magazine-style hero band
  • Science callouts + dinner-table facts

body.mode-learn

COOK mode

🔥 Functional checklist

For the how. Open on your phone at the stove.

  • Warm sunny yellow background
  • Checkboxes, big tap targets, mono qty
  • Step timers with ambient mood music
  • Zero prose between you and the step

body.mode-cook

Why the hard split: Recipe websites have ruined themselves with SEO-padded prose between you and the next step. When you're standing at the stove with one hand on a spatula, you should not be scrolling past the history of the egg.

Callouts

Note: Neutral aside. Used in lessons for "by the way" content.
Below ~62°C, egg whites are still liquid. Between 62°C and 70°C, proteins denature and reform as a soft mesh trapping water — silky curd. Past 70°C, the water is squeezed out: rubber.
The reason cold-start ramen eggs are jammy in the middle: the yolk sets at a lower temperature than the white. You're cooking two different things at once.
Heads up: Don't salt eggs more than five minutes before cooking — it pulls water out and you'll get weepy curds.

🎵 Kitchen mood — the timer audio

Generated, not licensed. Toggleable per page.

While any timer is running on a recipe card, a soft Cmaj9 pad hums underneath using Tone.js. When the timer hits zero, a clean three-note chime plays (C → E → G). No external audio files — it's all synthesized on demand and works offline. The user can mute it with the music toggle in the recipe page header.

Why a pad and not a melody: a melody would compete with the cook's own thoughts. A drone-ish pad just holds the room. The chime at the end is unmistakable but not jarring — a sound you'd hear in a real kitchen, not a notification ping.

Tone — calibration

Anchors so future-you doesn't drift toward AI-glaze.
👍 In voice:
"Low and slow. Take them off when they still look slightly underdone — they keep cooking from the pan's heat for about 20 seconds after."
👎 Off voice (too much glaze):
"Now, this is super important and you're going to love this technique — it's truly transformative and one of my favorite parts of the whole process!"
👎 Off voice (too clinical):
"Remove from heat at 80% denaturation to allow for residual thermal carryover."

Accessibility & device

What's been done; what's deliberate.
  • Primary device: Responsive — verified at 375px (phone), 768px (tablet), 1280px (laptop).
  • Touch targets ≥ 44px across all checkboxes, buttons, step rows — critical with one hand on a spatula.
  • No hover-only interactions.
  • Color is never the only signal. Done steps fade and show ✓.
  • Reduced motion respected via prefers-reduced-motion.
  • Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (warm dark brown on eggshell).
  • Audio is opt-out, not opt-in — but easy to mute, and never the only signal a timer finished (visual + animation also fire).
