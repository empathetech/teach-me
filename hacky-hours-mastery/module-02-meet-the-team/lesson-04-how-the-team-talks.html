How the team talks (chat modes)

You can decide how much of the team you actually hear. Sometimes you want one calm narrator; sometimes you want to watch the agents debate. That dial is called chat mode, and it has a real cost tradeoff worth understanding.

7 min read hands-on grows: cost awareness
First, a word on tokens AI models charge by tokens — roughly, chunks of text they read and write. More visible back-and-forth means more tokens, which means more cost. Chat mode is really a dial between "show me less, spend less" and "show me everything, spend more."

The three modes

Chat mode controls how visible the team is while it works. There are three settings, quoted here from the framework itself:

  • off — "single narrator, no team voices (cheapest)." One steady voice speaks for the whole team. Calmest, least expensive.
  • minimal — "speaker attribution at meaningful moments only." The team is mostly quiet, but individual agents speak up when it matters — a concern, a disagreement, a hand-off. No filler.
  • full — "closed-captioned multi-agent dialogue." You see the agents talk to each other, labelled by name, like reading a transcript of a meeting. Most vivid, most expensive.

The cost tradeoff

The framework is refreshingly honest about this. Turning on full mode runs what it calls "real per-role fan-out" — it genuinely asks each role to weigh in — and that "costs meaningfully more tokens than off or minimal."

The framework's own heads-up for full mode "Heads up: full mode runs real per-role fan-out and costs meaningfully more tokens than off or minimal. We don't have calibration data to quantify by how much… Switch back with /hacky-hours team chat minimal if it feels expensive."

So there's no single "best" mode. A common rhythm: keep minimal for everyday work, flip to full when you want to learn how the team reasons or you're making a big decision, and drop to off when you just want quiet, cheap output.

A cost dial, not a quality switch A quieter mode doesn't mean the work is sloppier, and a louder one doesn't guarantee it's better — it changes how much you see and spend, not whether the underlying decisions are sound. Reviewing the output still falls to you in every mode.

What you just learned

  • Chat mode sets how visible the team is: off (one narrator), minimal (speaks up when it matters), full (captioned multi-agent dialogue).
  • Full costs meaningfully more tokens; the framework warns you and tells you how to switch back.
  • Set it with /hacky-hours team chat <mode>; it applies to the next multi-role action.
  • Mode changes what you see and spend — not whether the underlying work is sound. Review still falls to you.
  • /hacky-hours team show <id> reads one agent regardless of chat mode.
