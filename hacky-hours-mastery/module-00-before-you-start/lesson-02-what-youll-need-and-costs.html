What you'll need, and what it costs

Before you install anything, here's the full picture — every requirement and every cost, laid out plainly. We'd rather you decide with eyes open than discover a price later.

What you need

The full list is short:

  • A computer running macOS 13+, Windows 10+ (1809 or newer), or a recent Linux, with at least 4 GB of RAM. A normal laptop from the last few years is fine.
  • An internet connection. Claude Code talks to Anthropic's servers to think.
  • A Claude account on a paid plan — this is the part that costs money. More on that just below.
  • Claude Code installed (Lesson 4) and the Hacky Hours skill installed (Lesson 5). Both are free to install; the cost is the plan that powers them.
You do not need: any coding knowledge, a powerful "gaming" computer, a credit card for the course itself, or to pay for hosting your finished apps (free options exist — see below).

The one real cost: a Claude plan

Here's the honest centre of it. Claude Code — and therefore Hacky Hours — requires a paid Claude plan. The free Claude.ai plan does not include Claude Code. You have a few ways to pay, and you can start at the bottom:

Prices in USD, as shown on Claude's pricing page in 2026. Always confirm the current price at claude.com/pricing — these change.
OptionCostGood for
Free $0 Chatting at claude.ai — but does not include Claude Code, so it won't run Hacky Hours.
Pro ~$20/month (or ~$17/month if you pay for a year up front) The usual starting point. Includes Claude Code. Plenty for learning and small projects.
Max From ~$100/month (5× or 20× the usage of Pro) Heavy or daily building, when you keep hitting Pro's limits.
Pay-as-you-go (API / Console) You load credits and pay per use People who'd rather pay only for what they use. More fiddly to set up than a plan.
We can't promise what it'll cost you Pricing, plan names, and usage limits are set by Anthropic and do change. The numbers above are a snapshot, not a guarantee — check claude.com/pricing for what's true today. How much you use is also up to you: a casual learner on Pro is very different from someone building all day. Start small; you can always upgrade.

What's actually free

So you can see where the money does and doesn't go:

  • This course — free, forever. It runs in your browser with no account.
  • Installing Claude Code — free. (The plan that powers it costs money.)
  • Installing the Hacky Hours skill — free and open-source.
  • Hosting the apps you build — free tiers exist (you'll meet these in Module 7). You don't pay to put a small project online.

The only thing you pay for is the Claude plan that does the thinking. Everything around it is free.

The bottom line

  • You need a normal computer, internet, and a paid Claude plan.
  • Pro (~$20/month) is the usual entry point and includes Claude Code.
  • The free plan won't run Hacky Hours; pay-as-you-go is an alternative to a subscription.
  • The course, the installs, and basic hosting are all free.
  • Prices change — always check claude.com/pricing.
