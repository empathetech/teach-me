Visual language

Grounded in Empathetech's own brand (empathetech.org): Poppins type, deep-purple with vibrant accents, generous rounding. Every page consumes one stylesheet — assets/theme.css — so a change here propagates everywhere. Tone: warm, plain-spoken, honest. No metaphors, no hype.

1 · Identity

Goal: use /hacky-hours confidently & keep growing as a real builder. Tone keywords: warm · encouraging · plain · honest · accessible. Voice rule: say things straight. We borrow Empathetech's joyful, inclusive feel and its commitment to accessibility — not cute theming.

6 · Tone example

You don't have to be a security expert to reduce common risks in what you build. But you do have to stay in the loop — because the responsibility for what your software exposes is, and always will be, yours. We'll build that judgment together, one small step at a time.

7 · Don't list

  • Don't put hardcoded colors, fonts, or pixel values in lesson HTML — use the tokens.
  • Don't write paragraphs longer than ~5 lines at prose width.
  • Don't use the metaphor voice ("conductor", "orchestra", "movements") — say things plainly.
  • Don't overpromise on security, privacy, or correctness — use .callout--responsibility.
  • Don't state a fact without a real, linkable source. Don't assume prior knowledge.

8 · Accessibility & device choices

Desktop-first (the skill runs in a terminal) but responsive to 375px. Baseline always on: semantic HTML, visible amber focus rings, contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for body text, color never the only signal, prefers-reduced-motion respected, keyboard-operable widgets. Accessibility is treated as a feature to celebrate, in keeping with Empathetech's mission.
